Report of the 5-weeks internship at Arcetri observatory

Conclusion

We have previously seen the basics of adaptive optics as well as the main sources that generate noise when taking a measurement by a camera. I also searched for a camera faster than the one they currently have to their provisions and that meets the criteria requested. This is the Sony ICX414 sensor, fitted to the Manta G-1236 camera, for a 1/770 arcsec/μm (See Annexe 1).

Then, I determined a range of diameter and vergence for a two-lens image shrinkage system. They must have a vergence between 112d and 1000d and a radius between 2.3mm and 205.6mm. I also did a simulation of a light signal reaching the camera and determined the radius needed to be able to have the best possible signal quality (see algorithm annexe 2)

Then, I studied the algorithm in Python language provided by Mr. Lorenzo BUSONI that allows to process the data acquired by the camera that the group has (see annexe 3). After having taken some pictures with the camera myself, I created my own algorithms in Python language (annexe 4) in order to process the information provided by them and I renamed them. There were 4240 all measures combined.

In addition to technical skills, I also developed my language skills, such as speaking in English or listening in Italian. Having a very good social climate, this research laboratory allowed me to spend a very pleasant internship and rich in personal development.

Finally, this internship allowed me to discover the working method used in this laboratory and which, I think, is used in many others. I realized the importance of checking a sample of results before processing all the data to avoid working with them if they are wrong or discovering as soon as possible the technical problems.
