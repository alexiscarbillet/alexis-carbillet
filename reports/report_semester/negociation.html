Report of 5 months exchange semester at ITMO university in Saint-Petersburg

Chapter 1 Negociation

Negotiation is a process of communication and exchange between at least two parties, the object of which concerns the organization of a relationship or the settlement of a problem between them.

Each class was composed of a theorical part at the beginning. Then a practical part was done, in small group. Its goal was to apply the knowledge given during the first part. There was negociation between two parties, three parties and at the final class, a negociation between six parties has been organized. The context was different each time. For example, it could be a customer who had a problem with the product he/she just bought, an agent who tried to get a contract for his actor or a problem between two departments of a company and its direction. It was interesting because each parties had different information so it was not possible to be totally prepare for the negociation, as in real life. All the negociatiions were in english. In total, more than 8 negociations have been done.

Different kind of negociations have been taught. First the Hardvard school of negociation, then the Kremlin school of negation and finally the Swedish school of negociation. Some books needed to be read like “Getting to Yes” by Roger Fisher and William L. Ury and also “Predictably Irrational: The Hidden Forces That Shape Our Decisions” by Dan Ariely. I discovered the concept of Zone of possible agreement (ZOPA) as well as the Best alternative to negotiated agreement (BATNA). I also learn how to profile people according to 4 categories (Dominant, Influencer, Compliant and Steady).
