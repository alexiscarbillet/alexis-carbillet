Report of 5 months exchange semester at ITMO university in Saint-Petersburg

Introduction

From the 1st of September 2019 to the 31st of January 2020 I did an exchange semester at ITMO University. In this introduction, I will first explain why, as a French, I chose to go in Russia. Then, I will talk about Saint-Petersburg and finally about the university.

Why did I choose Russia?

At first, french people, and other people, can find this choice surprising. I think so most surprised were the russian students. As an european student of data science, the expected choice is United States, Japan, China… but not Russia. Russian students want to go to Europe so they don’t understand why european student go to their country.

So why this choice? I studied, during my two first year of engineering school, russian. I wanted to improve it and it was the opportunity to do it. This is not the only reason. I am curious and I like travelling. In Europe, Russia has an image of rudeness and strong government. If you study the french history, you will notice that France and Russia have been allied during a lot of time. Some of the russian tsars were in love with France. They also defeated our great french emperor Napoleon 1st while France was dominating Europe from 1804 to 1814. They also fight with my country in the two world war. This participated to my decision.

I decided to study artificial intelligence and data science. Russia is one of the countries investing the most in artificial intelligence. Vladimir Putin has determined that “whoever becomes the leader in this field will become the master of the world” (Rapport de mission parlementaire, 2018, C.Villani). In fact, most of the russian think that the technology is the most important axis to be developped in their country. This semester was an opportunity to meet those people who might have an important job in this country later.

About Saint-Petersburg

5millions people live in this city, which is 10 times bigger than Paris. This difference of area is explained by the fact that there is no skyscraper in the city. This city the cultural capital of Russia. It was the capital of the country before the creation of the republic. There are around 250 museums and more than 40 theaters. It contains one of the biggest museum of the world, the hermitage museum. Its metro is one of the deepest of the world because of the rivers inside the city (mainly the Neva). It is also considered the most european city of Russia according to the numerous russian I talked to (most of them were from others russian cities, Moscow included). It is the second biggest city of Russia by its population, after Moscow where 12 millions people live. It is also is the second city in Europe by its surface and the fourth by its population within city limits according to Wikipedia.

I decided to go to this city instead of Moscow because the universities in Moscow only teach in russian according to the agreements they have with my school.

About ITMO University

ITMO University is one Russia’s leading higher education and research institutions, specializing in Information Technology, Optical Design and Engineering. It have many buildings in different part of the city. Classes can be held until 10pm and on Saturday too which is not the case in France. The university consists of 18 departments, 7 research institutes and one academy, with a total of 119 chairs. As of April 1, 2014, the total number of students is 13,890, with over 900 being foreign nationals. Their team won 7 times the International Collegiate Programming Contest and did a lot fo second and third place since 1999 which shows its high level in computer science. ITMO University has three museums: “Museum of ITMO University History”, “Museum of the Institute of Refrigeration and Biotechnologies” and “Museum of Optics”.
